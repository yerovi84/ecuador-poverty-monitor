Methodology and statistical quality

Trust begins with clear boundaries.

How the monitor separates analytical estimates, benchmark comparison and public communication.

The Ecuador Poverty Monitor makes its analytical status visible. It distinguishes the source data from local analytical products, curated publication outputs, benchmark comparisons, and the language used on the public site.

The core distinction

A benchmark comparison for analytical reproducibility checks whether a result aligns with a public reference under a stated tolerance.

It is not official institutional validation, certification, endorsement, or approval.

Purpose

What this monitor is

The monitor is a downstream analytical and communication layer built from public ENEMDU source files, explicit methodological contracts, reproducible processing with enemduR, curated aggregate outputs, and static Quarto pages.

It organizes results so readers can see what was estimated, for which period and domain, with which quality metadata, and against which public reference when a suitable benchmark is available.

Boundary

What this monitor is not

It is not an official statistical release, a substitute for INEC publications, or an independent statistical authority. It does not claim institutional validation. It also does not treat every analytical estimate as equally benchmarked or equally suitable for territorial disaggregation.

Data architecture

Four publication layers

1. Source microdata

Public ENEMDU files are processed locally. Microdata remain outside the public site and are not copied into versioned publication outputs.

2. Annual analytical outputs

Local derived products hold the analytical results required by the pipeline. They are separate from the website and are not intended as direct public interfaces.

3. Curated site outputs

Versionable publication files contain only the aggregate fields needed by Quarto. They exclude microdata, raw identifiers, private paths, and dynamic timestamps.

4. Quarto pages

Static pages consume curated outputs and add interpretation, benchmark labels, quality notes, and explicit limits without recalculating the underlying indicators.

Period contract

Three-speed architecture

Monthly

The monthly layer is a short-term pulse for national and urban-rural context, particularly labor-market conditions. It should not be read as a substitute for annual structural poverty analysis.

Quarterly

The quarterly layer supports intermediate urban monitoring, including the five main cities only when the survey design and configured domain contract support those estimates.

Annual

The annual layer is the structural core for income poverty, deprivation, multidimensional poverty, distribution, and broader territorial analysis. The current published poverty profiles use annual 2025 outputs.

Period and domain are linked: a territorial level is shown only when it is supported by the survey design for that period.

Reproducibility metadata

Benchmark status

benchmark_status records the result of a comparison with an identified public reference. It is a reproducibility field, not an institutional seal.

Benchmark status Meaning Public interpretation Current use in the site
Close match The analytical estimate falls within the stated comparison tolerance for the available public reference. The monitor closely reproduces that public benchmark under the documented analytical contract. Income poverty and extreme income poverty.
Not directly benchmarked No equivalent public annual table is used for a direct indicator-level comparison in this build. Read the result as a survey-weighted analytical estimate, not as a benchmark-matched headline. NBI and extreme NBI.
Outside tolerance / requires review A public reference is available, but the analytical comparison does not meet the current tolerance. The estimate may be published with a visible review flag, but it must not be described as a close reproduction. TPM and TPEM.
Reference missing or not matched The expected comparison cannot be completed for the relevant row or reference. Do not infer benchmark alignment; inspect the source and matching contract before making a claim. Reserved for pipeline transparency when applicable.

For the current annual income-poverty comparison, “close match” means a difference of no more than 0.5 percentage points from the public annual reference. That tolerance belongs to this analytical reproducibility check and does not imply official institutional validation.

Published portfolio

Current indicator status

Indicator What it measures Current benchmark interpretation
Income poverty Whether household per-capita income falls below the income-poverty line. Close match against the public annual benchmark.
Extreme income poverty Whether household per-capita income falls below the extreme-poverty line. Close match against the public annual benchmark.
NBI Structural deprivation measured through unmet basic needs. Not directly benchmarked against a public annual table in this build.
Extreme NBI More severe or accumulated unmet-basic-needs conditions under the implemented method. Not directly benchmarked against a public annual table in this build.
TPM Multidimensional poverty based on overlapping weighted deprivations and the implemented threshold. Benchmark comparison outside tolerance; requires methodological review.
TPEM Severe multidimensional poverty based on the implemented extreme threshold. Benchmark comparison outside tolerance; requires methodological review.

These indicators answer different questions. Income poverty concerns current monetary resources. NBI captures structural deficits in basic needs. TPM and TPEM summarize overlapping weighted deprivations. They should be read together, not treated as interchangeable poverty rates.

Estimation

Survey-weighted analytical estimates

The published indicators are survey-weighted analytical estimates. Survey weights translate sampled observations into estimates for the population represented by the valid survey domain.

  • weighted_n is the estimated population represented by the analytical denominator.
  • unweighted_n is the number of survey records contributing to that denominator.
  • quality_flag carries the available precision or representativity assessment from the analytical output.
  • benchmark_status describes benchmark comparison, which is separate from sample quality.

A large weighted_n does not by itself guarantee precision, and a benchmark close match does not replace review of sample size, domain validity, and quality flags.

Domains and universes

Read every estimate inside its contract

The current poverty pages publish national, urban, and rural annual domains. Each estimate also belongs to a defined universe and analysis unit. Readers should not compare or combine rows without checking that period, universe, analysis unit, and domain are compatible.

More detailed geography is not automatically more informative. City, province, or other territorial estimates should appear only in the layers and periods where the survey design supports them. Unsupported disaggregation can create false precision even when the underlying calculation runs successfully.

How to read a quality flag

A quality flag is a decision aid, not decoration. If the field is available, read its value before publishing or interpreting a subgroup estimate. A restrictive or review-oriented flag should lead to qualification, suppression, or further methodological inspection according to the producing method. If the field is unavailable, the absence of a warning must not be interpreted as evidence of high precision.

Communication standard

Public language contract

The site may say that an estimate is:

  • based on public ENEMDU microdata;
  • a survey-weighted analytical estimate;
  • compared against public annual INEC tabulations;
  • a close benchmark match under the documented tolerance;
  • not directly benchmarked in this build; or
  • outside tolerance and requiring methodological review.

The site must not imply certification, institutional approval, endorsement, or formal validation by INEC. Benchmark alignment documents analytical reproducibility only.

Honest limits

Current limitations

  • This monitor is not an official statistical release and does not carry institutional validation.
  • NBI and extreme NBI do not have a direct public annual benchmark inside the current build.
  • TPM and TPEM require methodological review before they can be described as close reproductions of the public reference.
  • The current annual pages focus on national, urban, and rural domains; finer disaggregation requires a separate representativity contract.
  • Future territorial and labor layers require their own curated outputs, quality checks, and publication rules.
  • Static publication improves auditability, but it does not remove the need to review methods when source files, survey design, or official references change.

Separated layers

Microdata, local analysis, curated outputs, and public pages have distinct roles.

Visible status

Benchmark and quality metadata remain part of the public interpretation.

Valid domains

Disaggregation follows the period-specific representativity contract.

Bounded claims

Analytical reproducibility is never presented as institutional validation.
