Income poverty

Ecuador’s 2025 annual poverty profile

The first analytical layer of the monitor turns the annual ENEMDU poverty output into a compact reading of income, territory, and reproducibility.

In 2025, Ecuador’s annual income poverty rate reached 21.95%. The national figure is the starting point, but the substantive story is territorial: poverty reached 16.51% in urban areas and 33.63% in rural areas.

First published analytical layer

This page uses the curated site output data/derived/site/site_kpis.rds. It presents aggregated indicators only: no raw microdata, no private paths, and no individual identifiers.

2025 annual reading

Income poverty

The annual estimate shows that roughly one in five people lived in households whose per-capita income did not reach the income poverty line. The national rate was 21.95%.

The monitor treats this figure as an annual structural reading, not as a monthly pulse. That distinction matters because the annual ENEMDU file supports a broader territorial and thematic analysis than short-term releases.

Territorial contrast

Urban-rural gap

The urban-rural contrast is the strongest signal in this first layer. Urban income poverty reached 16.51%, while rural income poverty reached 33.63%.

That gap is not only a difference in levels. It points to different policy questions: labor income and cost of living in urban areas, and a more structural combination of income generation, access, and territorial vulnerability in rural areas.

Extreme poverty

A deeper income threshold

Extreme income poverty reached 6.99% nationally in 2025. The territorial contrast is again sharp: 3.69% in urban areas and 14.07% in rural areas.

This layer separates income poverty from extreme income poverty because the two indicators answer different questions. Poverty identifies households below the income poverty line; extreme poverty identifies households below a more restrictive minimum threshold.

Estimates are computed from public ENEMDU microdata and compared against public annual INEC tabulations for analytical reproducibility. This is not an official institutional validation.

Reproducibility

Benchmark comparison

The annual output was compared against public 2025 annual INEC tabulations. The published benchmark values are close to the monitor estimates after rounding: 21.8% for national income poverty, 16.3% for urban income poverty, 33.4% for rural income poverty, 6.8% for national extreme poverty, 3.6% for urban extreme poverty, and 13.7% for rural extreme poverty.

The comparison is a reproducibility check against a public reference table. It is not an institutional endorsement or a replacement for official statistical releases.

Interpretation limits

What this page does not claim

This page does not estimate causality, explain every driver of poverty, or create a new official statistic. It presents a stable annual analytical layer built from a derived, aggregated site output.

The next versions of the monitor should add uncertainty, representativity notes, and deeper territorial detail only where the survey design supports those domains.

Next analytical layers

The next income-poverty layer can add poverty-line context, precision indicators, historical comparisons, and distributional readings. Deprivation, multidimensional poverty, labor, and province-level analysis should remain separate layers until their own output contracts are complete.
