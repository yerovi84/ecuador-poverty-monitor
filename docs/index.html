2025 annual income poverty snapshot

In 2025, Ecuador’s annual income poverty rate reached 21.95%. The annual reading shows a strong territorial contrast: 16.51% in urban areas and 33.63% in rural areas. Extreme poverty reached 6.99% nationally, with 3.69% in urban areas and 14.07% in rural areas.

These estimates are computed from public ENEMDU microdata and compared against public annual INEC tabulations for analytical reproducibility. This is not an official institutional validation.

Editorial purpose

Why this monitor exists

Public poverty data in Ecuador are valuable, but the reading is often spread across bulletins, microdata, technical notes, methodological reports, and isolated tables.

The problem is not the absence of data. The problem is that the story connecting the data is often fragmented.

The Ecuador Poverty Monitor is designed as a reproducible communication layer. It does not replace official statistics or create a parallel statistical authority. It helps readers understand what is measured, how it is measured, what can be compared, and where each estimate has limits.

Reading framework

The core idea

A useful poverty monitor should not only ask how many people are poor.

Condition

Income, deprivation, and the relative position of households.

Place

Territory, cities, rurality, and valid domains of estimation.

Measurement

Precision, representativity, and comparability across periods.
