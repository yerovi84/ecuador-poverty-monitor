# Labor and poverty product design

## Public page objective

The future labor page should become the fourth public layer of the Ecuador Poverty Monitor roadmap. Its job is to explain how employment conditions and poverty intersect without implying causality or replacing official labor-market releases.

Core public question:

> Why having work does not always mean being out of poverty?

The page should show that labor-market attachment, employment quality, household income poverty and income distribution need to be read together.

## Intended audience

- international portfolio reviewers;
- policy and development audiences interested in Ecuador;
- Ecuadorian readers who need a clear non-technical interpretation of ENEMDU labor and poverty evidence;
- technical reviewers who need enough transparency to trust the public story, without seeing internal contracts in the page body.

## Recommended page title

Primary option:

> Labor market and poverty

Subtitle:

> Employment conditions and household vulnerability in annual ENEMDU 2025

Alternative title:

> Work is not always a shield from poverty

## Narrative arc

1. Open with the central tension: work matters, but not every job protects a household from poverty.
2. Show the labor-market structure: working-age population, labor force, employed, unemployed and outside labor force.
3. Move from work/no work to work quality: adequate/full employment, subemployment, other non-full employment and unpaid work if available.
4. Connect labor categories to income poverty and extreme income poverty.
5. Add income quintiles to show how labor conditions are distributed across the income ladder.
6. Close with interpretation limits: these are analytical profiles and associations, not causal estimates.

## Proposed sections

### 1. Hero / opening

Purpose: introduce the page as an executive labor-poverty reading.

Possible visible copy:

> Having work is central to household wellbeing, but employment alone does not guarantee that a household is above the income-poverty line.

### 2. Labor-market structure

Purpose: show how the population is distributed across labor-force states.

Expected elements:

- compact flow or stacked bar for working-age population;
- labor force vs outside labor force;
- employed vs unemployed.

### 3. Employment quality

Purpose: move beyond unemployment.

Expected elements:

- adequate/full employment;
- subemployment;
- other non-full employment if available;
- unpaid employment if available;
- short explanatory note that these categories depend on confirmed ENEMDU definitions.

### 4. Poverty by labor condition

Purpose: answer the central public question.

Expected elements:

- table or bar chart of income-poverty rates by broad labor condition;
- extreme poverty as a secondary measure;
- estimated people in poverty by labor condition if quality supports it.

### 5. Formality and poverty

Purpose: show whether the formal/informal divide adds an important vulnerability signal.

Expected elements:

- only publish if a defensible formal/informal definition is confirmed;
- compare poverty rates and estimated counts by formal/informal employment;
- avoid presenting informality as a moral or causal label.

### 6. Income quintiles by labor condition

Purpose: connect labor conditions to the distributional layer.

Expected elements:

- heatmap or compact table crossing labor condition with income quintile;
- explanatory copy separating income distribution from poverty thresholds.

### 7. Reading guide and source note

Purpose: keep the public page prudent without making it technical.

Expected copy:

> Source: author's calculations based on public ENEMDU annual microdata. Estimates are analytical and should be read with the methodology and statistical quality page.

## Proposed visuals and tables

| Visual/table | Public question | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Labor structure flow | Who is in the labor force, employed, unemployed or outside the labor force? | Use broad categories and avoid variable names. |
| Employment quality stacked bar | What share of employed people are in adequate/full employment, subemployment or other categories? | Only categories confirmed in real data. |
| Poverty by labor condition bars | Which labor conditions are associated with higher poverty rates? | Include caution when subgroup estimates are unstable. |
| Executive table | Where are the largest estimated poor populations by labor condition? | Rank by estimated people only after publication rules are applied. |
| Formality comparison | Is informal employment associated with higher poverty? | Include only if formal/informal definition is defensible. |
| Quintile heatmap | How are labor conditions distributed across income quintiles? | Depends on annual quintile output. |

## What the page should not include

- internal variable names such as `condact`, `condactn`, `p20`, `p21`, `p22`, `p32`, `p34` or `p35`;
- output schema tables;
- private paths or file names for raw microdata;
- technical validation logs;
- a claim that the monitor's estimates are official;
- a claim that employment status causes poverty;
- a new labor classification invented by the monitor;
- province or canton labor-poverty maps in the first public version;
- unsupported city comparisons.

## Relation to roadmap

Current roadmap:

1. Income
2. Deprivation
3. Territory
4. Labor
5. Measurement

The labor page fills the fourth promised layer. It should not compete with the income-poverty page; instead, it should explain why poverty analysis needs labor quality, employment status and income distribution in the same reading frame.

The measurement layer should remain the place for benchmark status, quality metadata, domains and public-language boundaries. The labor page should link there discreetly rather than reproduce technical contracts.

## Editorial tone

The public page should feel:

- professional;
- executive;
- visual;
- sober;
- internationally readable;
- grounded in public ENEMDU evidence;
- clear about limitations without sounding like an audit document.

Preferred verbs:

- shows;
- suggests;
- is associated with;
- is linked to;
- helps distinguish;
- should be read together with.

Avoid verbs:

- proves;
- causes;
- certifies;
- validates;
- ranks officially.

## Definition of done for page phase 4O

The public page phase is complete when:

- a curated site labor output exists;
- the page consumes only aggregate site output;
- no microdata are exposed;
- no internal variable names appear in visible page text;
- visuals distinguish labor-force status, employment quality, poverty and quintiles;
- the page links to income, deprivation, territory and measurement/statistical quality;
- no official or causal claim is made;
- the site renders successfully;
- search index does not surface internal contract language from the public page.
